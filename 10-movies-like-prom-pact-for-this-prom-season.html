10 Movies Like Prom Pact For This Prom Season

Publish date: 2024-05-28

Movies like Prom Pact are 1. The Kissing Booth 2. 10 Things I Hate About You 3. To All The Boys I Have Loved Before and 7 others.

Prom night can be called one of the celebrated days for any high school student. And many try their best not to ruin their prom night at all costs.

Likewise, in the movies, especially in teen rom-coms, their high school always ends with a prom night. Sometimes the whole movie even revolves around the prom as the main focus.

These moments will last for longer days as this is a special night for any high schooler. And this provides the filmmakers with the perfect materials for filmmaking as they show the transition of a high school student to an adult and making their choices.

So, to let you get up the spirit of prom season, we have brought the list of 10 movies you can binge-watch or on your next sleepover with your friends.

10. Never Been Kissed

  • Rating: 6.0
  • Genre: Comedy, Drama, Romance
  • Cast Members: Drew Barrymore, Michael Vartan, Molly Shannon, John C. Reilly and Jessica Alba
  • Duration: 1 hour 47 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Hulu, Disney+, NBC, SYFY, Bravo, Oxygen, Prime Video, Vudu and E!

The movie has been produced by Fox 2000 Pictures, Bushwood Pictures and Flower Films while Twentieth Century Fox was one of the main distributors for the movie all around the globe. The movie was released on April 9, 1999, in the United States.

Never Been Kissed is an American romantic comedy movie which was directed by Raka Gosnell while the movie story was excellently written by Abby Kohn and Marc Silverstein. The movie follows the story of the protagonist Josie Geller.

Josie Geller played by Drew Barrymore is an insecure girl who is currently working as a copy editor for the Chicago Sun-Times. But she is assigned to go undercover at a high school by her editor-in-chief.

Josie who used to have a geek persona had somehow destroyed her first high school in her high school days. So, to not make her obnoxious, she tries to befriend three popular high school girls.

In the process, she falls in love with her literary teacher.

9. He’s All That

  • Rating: 4.4
  • Genre: Comedy, Romance
  • Cast Members: Addison Rae, Tanner Buchman, Maddison Pettis, Rachael Leigh Cook and Matthew Lillard
  • Duration: 1 hour 28 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Netflix and Plex TV

The movie has been produced under the guidance of Ethea Entertainment, Miramax and Netflix. The movie’s right to distribution has been given to Netflix.

The movie was filmed at Ulysses S. Grant High School in Los Angeles. The movie was released on August 27, 2021.

The director of the movie is Mark Waters and R. Lee Fleming Jr. has upheld the competent writer for the movie. The movie is the remake of a 1999 film called She’s All That with a modern adaptation included in the mix.

The movie follows the story of a young TikTok influencer who is in her final year of high school. Though she lives with her divorced, nurse mother, she tells different stories to her followers and sponsors on her account.

But while surprising her boyfriend on her live stream, she finds him cheating on her. Enraged she outbursts on the live stream showing her angered and snot-filled face.

To redeem her followers and sponsors, she takes the challenge of making the unpopular man into prom king.

8. Senior Year

  • Rating: 5.5
  • Genre: Comedy and Drama
  • Cast Members: Rebel Wilson, Angourie Rice, Mary Holland, Molly Brown and Sam Richardson
  • Duration: 1 hour 51 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Netflix

The production house involved in producing Senior Year is Broken Road Productions, CBS Films and Paramount Pictures. While the right for the movies to be distributed worldwide was given to Netflix.

The direction of the movie was handled by Alex Hardcastle and Andrew Knauer, Arthur Pielli and Brandon Scott Jones were the scriptwriter for the movie.

The movie is an American comedy that features a story of a woman who awakens after 20 years in a coma.

The movie follows the story of Stephanie, who is the captain of the cheer squad and has the plan to win the title of prom queen that she always dreamed of. But during her cheer performance, her landing is spoiled by her boyfriend’s ex-girlfriend, sending her to a coma.

Fast-forward 20 years, Stephanie wakes up from her ailment and she finds everything has changed vastly. Since she never completed high school graduation, she decided to complete her graduation.

But she is not content with completing her graduation but also being a prom queen. Will she be able to complete her dream?

7. Mean Girls

  • Rating: 7.1
  • Genre: Comedy
  • Cast Members: Lindsay Lohan, Jonathan Bennett, Rachel McAdams, Tina Fey and Tim Meadows
  • Duration: 1 hour 37 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Showtime, Paramount+, Amazon Instant Video, Vudu and Pluto

The production house involved in producing Mean Girls is Paramount Pictures M.G. Films and Broadway Video. While the distribution right for the movie was given to Paramount Pictures and Bontonfilm.

The direction of the movie was handled by Mark Waters and Rosalind Wisemen and Tina Fey wrote the script for the movie.

This teen comedy movie was based on Rosalind’s 2002 book called Queen Bees and Wannabees and Fey used her experience of her high school days.

Mean Girls follow the story of sixteen-year-old Cady Heron who has spent more than twelve years in Africa. Now coming back to the US, she starts her school at North Shore High School. She is befriended by the school’s outsiders but they tell her to avoid the Plastics groups.

Cady infiltrates the group to sabotage Regina’s wardrobe to damage Regina’s reputation. But she herself turns into another Regina upsetting all the people near her. Will she be able to make everything right?

6. The Kissing Booth

  • Rating: 5.9
  • Genre: Comedy and Romance
  • Cast Members: Joey King, Jacob Elordi, Joel Courtney, Stephen Jennings and Hilton Pelser
  • Duration: 1 hour 45 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Netflix

The movie The Kissing Booth was produced by Netflix, Komixx Entertainment and Generation Studios. Most of the right for this movie distribution was provided to Netflix. The movie was released on May 11, 2018, in the United States

The movie was directed by Vince Marcello and the screenplay was written by Vince Marcello and Beth Reekles. The story follows a late-blooming teenager who has a crush on her lifelong friend’s big brother but is afraid to be open about it. She doesn’t want her friend to know as that will put an end to their friendship.

Elle and her childhood friend Lee have been best friends since their birth. But Lee also has a brother Noah who is older than him and Elle who is budding into her teenage years is love-struck with Noah. But initially, she tries to suppress it.

Elle and Lee propose a kissing booth at the school event as a fundraiser. Lee gets swept by Rachel and two leave her behind. In the blindfold event, she also participates and gets her luck shot with Noah kissing him.

5. Lady Bird

  • Rating: 7.4
  • Genre: Drama and Comedy
  • Cast Members: Saoirse Ronan, Laurie Metcalf, Tracy Letts, Lucas Hedges and Timothee Chalamet
  • Duration: 1 hour 34 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Hulu and Disney+, Paramount Plus, Prime Video, Apple TV and ESPN+

The movie Lady Bird has been produced by IAC Films, Scott Rudin Productions and Entertainment 360. The movie has been distributed by A24, Columbia Pictures and Elevation Pictures.

The movie was released on December 1, 2017, in the United States.

The movie’s screenwriter and direction have both been created by Greta Gerwig which is her solo directorial movie debut. The movie tells the story of a high schooler who has tensions filled relationship with her mother but reconciles at the end.

Lady Bird follows the story of Christine in the fall of 2002 when she is in her senior year of Mary Heart Catholic High School. She is quite a rebellious child who has an estranged relationship with her mother.

To show her rebellion, she has given herself the nickname Lady Bird. She wants to attend a prestigious college somewhere on East Coast but her family’s financial situation is a bit weak to send her to those places.

4. Sierra Burgess Is A Loser

  • Rating: 5.8
  • Genre: Comedy, Romance and Drama
  • Cast Members: Shannon Purser, Kristine Froseth, RJ Cyler, Noah Centineo and Loretta Devine
  • Duration: 1 hour 45 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Netflix

The production house involved in producing Sierra Burgess Is A Loser is “Black Label Media” and the distribution right for the movie is given to Netflix. The movie was released on September 7, 2018, in the United States.

The American teen comedy-drama movie was beautifully directed by Ian Samuels and the movie scriptwriter is Lindsey Beer. The movie is a modern retelling of the 1897 play.

The movies focus on the life of Sierra who is smart and aspirational and trying to get herself admitted into Stanford University. But she is not the popular and attractive persona in her school premise which leads her to be bullied sometime.

Jamie is the reputed football player in the school and wants to date Veronica but she is already dating someone else. So, Veronica gives Sierra’s instead of hers to dodge Jamie’s attention.

Though Jamie and Sierra start texting each other, Sierra gets to know who Jamie is but Jamie still thinks he is talking to Veronica. But that doesn’t stop her from having crush on him.

  • Rating: 6.3
  • Genre: Comedy, Romance and Drama
  • Cast Members: Lana Condor, Noah Centineo, Janel Parrish, Anna Cathcart and Ross Butler
  • Duration: 1 hour 49 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Netflix

Meanwhile Netflix is the one that holds the distribution right for the movie. The movie was released on February 12, 2021.

The movie has been directed by Michael Fimognari and Katie Lovejoy, Jenny Han and Maxwell Peters are the scriptwriters for the movie.

The movie is the third franchise and the sequel of the movie To All The Boys: P.S. I Still Love You and the final installment to the series.

The movie follows the story of Lara Jean similar to the movie’s first and second sequels. In this sequel, she along with her family and her neighbor goes to Seoul for Lara to connect with her mother’s memory in her memory of her father.

2. 10 Things I Hate About You

  • Rating: 7.3
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy and Drama
  • Cast Members: Heath Ledger, Julia Stiles, Joseph Gordon-Levitt, Larisa Oleynik and David Krumholtz
  • Duration: 1 hour 37 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Disney +, Freeform, Hulu, Prime Video, Vudu and Netflix

The movie 10 Things I Hate About You has been produced by Touchstone Pictures, Mad Chance and Jaret Entertainment. The movie distribution right was provided to Buena Vista Pictures and Buena Vista International. The movie was released on March 31, 1999.

The movie has been directed by Gil Junger and the movie scriptwriter includes Karen McCullah and Kirsten Smith. The movie is inspired by William Shakespeare’s well-known play The Taming of Shrew and has been modernized it.

The movie starts with the introduction of Cameron James who is a new student at his school. And on his first day, he falls in love with Bianca Stratford at a first glance. But he learns that her father has forbidden both her and her sister to date in high school.

Meanwhile, the older sister Kat is accepted into a college in New York but her father doesn’t want that. Seeing his daughter’s insistence and rebelliousness he declares that Bianca can only date if Kat dates someone.

1. The Kissing Booth 2

  • Rating: 5.7
  • Genre: Comedy and Romance
  • Cast Members: Joey King, Joel Courtney, Jacob Elordi, Molly Ringwald and Taylor Zakhar Perez
  • Duration: 2 hours 14 minutes
  • Where To Watch: Netflix

The production houses involved in producing The Kissing Booth are Netflix, Picture Loom, Clearbanks Films and Komixx Entertainment. The movie distribution was done by Netflix. The movie was released on July 24, 2020, in the United States.

This movie has been directed by Vince Marcello and the writer for the movie are Vince Marcello, Jay S Arnold and Beth Reekles.

This movie is the sequel to The Kissing Booth in 2018 and is the second installment of the Kissing Booth trilogy.

The movie show’s that Elle is in her senior year and Noah is spending his days at his university.

Due to their long-distance relationship, there is much gossip about them breaking up. Plus her fear for their relationship rises when Noah gets along with a British girl named Chloe.

Now in the new year, they again organize the kissing booth for the Charity Fair. For this year, Lee and Elle try to convince the new student named Marco to be the kisser for the booth. Will the relationship change along with the change of kisser of the booth?
