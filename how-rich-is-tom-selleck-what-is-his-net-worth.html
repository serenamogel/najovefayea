Who is Tom Selleck?

Thomas William Selleck was born on January 29, 1945 in Detroit, Michigan, United States. His parents were Robert D. Selleck and Martha S. Jagger. He had three siblings; Daniel Selleck, Martha Selleck, Robert Selleck.

He had his college education at Los Angeles Valley College and further studied at University of Southern California.

From 1967 to 1973, Tom served in the 160th Infantry Regiment of the California Army National upon receiving a draft notice during the Vietnam War. Tom had his first cameo in 1969 and since then, he has been involved with a whole host of movies.

Selleck has been in numerous movies, including “Three Men and a Baby,” “Quigley Down Under,” and “Mr. Baseball.”

His breakthrough movie, however, was Magnum P.I. from 1980 to 1988 where he played the role of a private investigator and received five Emmy Award nominations.

Tom has won awards for the years for his outstanding performances in his roles in the Television Series. Favorite Actor in a New TV Series, Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series, and Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Television Series Drama.

For his great works, Tom received an Honorary Doctorate Degree from Pepperdine University in April 28, 2000. He was also honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
