Meesha Shafi files Rs2bn defamation suit against Ali Zafar

Publish date: 2024-03-14
A well-known Pakistani Singer Meesha Shafi has filed Rs2 billion defamation suit in a Lahore sessions court against Pakistani Singer Ali Zafar for making false claims against her on media, it rose on Wednesday. The defamation suit incorporates Rs1bn for the loss of generosity and notoriety caused, and Rs1bn for “mental torment and misery”. Notwithstanding the damages mentioned, Meesha Shafi requested that the court pass a declaration to announce Zafar’s statements seeing her as “false, vindictive and slanderous” and “made to harm the notoriety of the offended party”. “It is asked that the respondent (Ali Zafar) be for all time-limited from owning any further disparaging expression against the offended party (Meesha Shafi),” the suit expressed, including that the abusive explanations had made a misfortune Meesha Shafi’s generosity and antagonistically influenced her. “In addition, it has made mental misery and anguish the offended party and for which [the] litigant is presently at risk to pay harms,” it included. Shafi’s group had also sent a lawful notice to Zafar for maligning in May. Prior, Ali Zafar had presented Rs1bn harms suit against Meesha Shafi for blaming him for lewd behavior. The case is at present in progress in a sessions courts in Lahore. Zafar made the legitimate move after Meesha Shafi blamed him for pestering her “on multiple events”.
