Top 6 Models In Africa Changing The Face Of Fashion

Publish date: 2024-08-21

When it comes to models in Africa it boasts beautiful women from diverse cultural backgrounds

And many of them have successfully transformed how people perceive beauty.

Here, we present a list of the top 6 African models who have shattered stereotypes, making the continent proud.

1. Maria Borges: Angola

Maria Borges, an Angolan model, stormed into the fashion world, gracing numerous high-profile shows such as Victoria’s Secret Fashion Show, Givenchy, Balmain, and Chanel.

With her stunning looks and distinctive short Afro hair, Maria has graced the covers of many magazines, including Vogue, Harper’s Bazaar, and Elle.

2. Nyadak Thot: South Sudan

Duckie Thot, the South Sudanese-Australian model celebrated for her striking beauty and unique features.

Has graced the catwalks of renowned designers like Fenty Beauty, Oscar de la Renta, and Moschino.

Additionally, Duckie has been featured in numerous magazines, including Vogue, Paper, and Grazia.

3. Victoria Michaels: Ghana

Victoria Michaels, a Ghanaian model, entrepreneur, and philanthropist, initiated her modeling career in 2004.

And has since risen to prominence as one of the African fashion industry’s most recognizable figures.

Collaborating with renowned designers like Ozwald Boateng and Christie Brown, Victoria has graced the pages of esteemed publications such as Vanity Fair and Elle.

Beyond her modeling pursuits, Victoria serves as the founder of the Victoria Michaels Foundation,

dedicated to empowering young women in Ghana through educational and mentorship programs.

4. Fatima Siad: Somali/Ethiopian

Fatima Siad, a Somali-Ethiopian model, catapulted to fame when she competed on America’s Next Top Model in 2008.

Since then, she has collaborated with prestigious fashion brands like Ralph Lauren, H&M, and Armani.

Her presence has graced the pages of magazines like Elle and Vogue, and she has graced catwalks worldwide.

Beyond her modeling pursuits, Fatima champions diversity in the fashion industry and has vocally criticized the underrepresentation of Black models.

She leverages her platform to inspire and advocate for inclusivity within the industry.

5. Flaviana Matata: Tanzania

Flaviana Matata is a Tanzanian model and beauty pageant winner who has become one of the most successful models in Africa.

She has walked for designers such as Marc Jacobs, Tory Burch, and Oscar de la Renta.

Flaviana has also been featured in magazines such as Elle, Harper’s Bazaar, and Cosmopolitan.

6. Malaika Firth – Kenya/British

Malaika Firth, a Kenyan-British model, owes her career to her mother’s initiative in contacting Premier Models in London.

With a diverse heritage, Malaika welcomes discussions about her race, considering herself a junior ambassador.

She made history as the second black model in a Prada ad, following Naomi Campbell in 1994, and has emerged as a rising star in the modeling world at just 21.

Based in New York, she is represented by Premier Models.

All of the beauties on this list represent their homeland very well with the title of top .

Africa is home to a diverse array of beautiful women who have challenged conventional beauty standards.
